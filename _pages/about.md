I am a PhD Candidate in Strategic Management and Public Policy at the George Washington University School of Business.
I study how workers and organizations adapt to environmental disruption in resource-constrained contexts.
My dissertation examines how workers in Nepal’s Himalayan trekking industry navigate climate change. Using grounded theory, I draw on precarity and extreme context literatures to explore how porters and guides sustain their livelihoods under conditions of extreme precarity. I also examine how trekking workers, community members, and conservation actors negotiate and contest adaptive responses to climate change within the place-based context of Himalayan trekking.
